Water Treatment Plants (WTPs)

Water Treatment Plants play a vital role in ensuring the supply of clean and safe water to residential societies. They remove impurities like bacteria, viruses, heavy metals, and other dissolved solids, ensuring the water is safe for drinking, cooking, and other household uses.

Types of Water Treatment Plants

Physical Treatment Plants

Use sedimentation, filtration, and flotation to remove suspended solids and impurities.

Chemical Treatment Plants

Employ chlorination, coagulation, and flocculation to eliminate microorganisms and other contaminants.

Biological Treatment Plants

Utilize activated sludge and trickling filters to remove organic pollutants.

Desalination Plants

Designed to remove salts and dissolved solids from saline water sources, such as seawater or brackish groundwater.

Key Parameters to Monitor

  • Turbidity: Ensures water clarity and effective disinfection.
  • pH Level: Ideal range between 6.5 and 8.5 for safe drinking water.
  • Total Dissolved Solids (TDS): Measures combined content of minerals, salts, and metals.
  • Heavy Metals: Toxic elements like lead, arsenic, and mercury.
  • Microbiological Contaminants: Includes harmful pathogens like E. coli and coliform bacteria.

Maintenance and Calibration

Regular maintenance and calibration of WTPs are essential for optimal performance. This includes cleaning filters, replacing worn-out parts, and monitoring the system's sensors to ensure accurate results. Maintenance costs may vary depending on the type and scale of the treatment plant.
